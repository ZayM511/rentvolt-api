# Refund Policy

**Version:** 2026.04.17
**Effective Date:** April 17, 2026

## 1. Summary

- **14-day refund on your first charge** for paid subscriptions (Growth, Scale, Enterprise).
- Subscriptions are otherwise **non-refundable** except as required by law.
- Free plan has nothing to refund.
- **Cancel anytime** to stop future charges; access continues through the end of the paid period.

## 2. 14-Day First-Charge Refund (Good-Faith Guarantee)

If you subscribe to a paid plan for the first time and are dissatisfied, you may request a full refund of your first monthly charge within 14 days of the charge date. After a refund, your subscription is cancelled and your API key is downgraded to the Free plan (or deactivated, at our discretion).

**How to request.** Email `support@groundworklabs.io` from your account email with the word **Refund** in the subject. Include your Stripe receipt number if available. We will respond within 5 business days.

The 14-day refund applies only to a first subscription. If you cancel and resubscribe, subsequent first-month charges are not eligible.

## 3. Cancellation (No Refund Required)

You may cancel at any time via:

- The `/dashboard` page (self-serve one-click)
- Stripe's customer portal
- Emailing `support@groundworklabs.io`

Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period. You retain access through the paid period; no further charges occur. We do not provide refunds for partial months other than under Section 2 or where required by law.

## 4. Service-Disruption Credits

If we experience unplanned downtime exceeding 4 consecutive hours in a calendar month, we will, upon request, issue a prorated credit equal to the portion of that month during which the Service was unavailable. Email `support@groundworklabs.io` with the incident time and your account email.

## 5. Statutory Rights

Nothing in this policy limits any non-waivable refund or cancellation rights you may have under California Business & Professions Code § 17602 (California Automatic Renewal Law), California Civil Code § 1750 et seq. (Consumer Legal Remedies Act), or comparable state law. If you believe a charge was unauthorized, you may dispute it directly with your card issuer.

## 6. Disputes

If we decline a refund request and you disagree, you may escalate via Stripe's dispute process or use the dispute-resolution procedure in the [Terms of Service](/legal/tos) Section 13.
