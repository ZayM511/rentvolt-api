# Acceptable Use Policy

**Version:** 2026.04.17
**Effective Date:** April 17, 2026

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") governs your use of the RentVolt API and Data. Violations may result in suspension or termination of your account. The AUP is part of, and incorporated into, the [Terms of Service](/legal/tos).

## 1. No Housing Discrimination

**You may not use the Service or Data to discriminate against any person on the basis of:**

- Race, color, national origin, or ancestry
- Religion
- Sex (including gender identity, gender expression, and sexual orientation)
- Familial status (including presence of children)
- Disability
- Marital status
- Source of income (including Section 8 / housing vouchers)
- Veteran or military status
- Citizenship or immigration status
- Age (where protected)
- Any other class protected under the Fair Housing Act (42 U.S.C. §§ 3601–3619), California Fair Employment and Housing Act (Gov't Code § 12900 et seq.), California Civil Code § 51 (Unruh Act), or other applicable law.

This prohibition includes:

- Filtering, ranking, excluding, steering, or targeting listings in ways that produce a disparate impact on protected classes.
- Using Data to advertise housing in a manner that indicates a preference, limitation, or discrimination based on a protected class.
- Using Data to refuse, delay, or modify offers of housing based on a protected class.
- Displaying Data in products that enable such discrimination.

**Violations will result in immediate suspension and may be referred to regulators.**

## 2. No Abuse of the Service

You may not:

- Circumvent or attempt to circumvent rate limits, quotas, or access controls.
- Share, sell, or transfer API keys to third parties.
- Use multiple accounts to evade limits.
- Attempt to reverse engineer, disassemble, or decompile the Service.
- Probe, scan, or test the vulnerability of the Service without written permission.
- Introduce malware, viruses, or other harmful code.
- Interfere with or disrupt the Service or its infrastructure.
- Use the Service for denial-of-service attacks or to send spam.

## 3. No Bulk Redistribution or Competing Service

You may not:

- Redistribute raw bulk datasets derived from the Data (e.g., selling a dump of property records).
- Offer a substantially similar API service whose primary purpose is redistributing the Data.
- Train machine-learning models on bulk exports of the Data without a separate written license.

You **may** build products and services that display the Data to end users (e.g., apps, dashboards, reports), provided such products do not primarily function as Data redistribution.

## 4. Lawful Use of Contact Information

If a listing returned by the Service includes contact information (phone, email, agent name), you may use it only for bona fide, individualized property inquiries. You may not:

- Scrape, bulk-harvest, or compile contact information.
- Use contact information for cold outreach, marketing, or unsolicited commercial messages.
- Violate the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA), the CAN-SPAM Act, or Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 17529.5.

## 5. No Unlawful Use

You may not use the Service or Data to violate any law or regulation, including (without limitation) laws regarding money laundering, terrorism financing, sanctions compliance, export controls, unfair or deceptive acts or practices, false advertising, securities fraud, or consumer protection.

## 6. Enforcement

- We may investigate suspected AUP violations and may access account data as needed.
- We may suspend or terminate access without refund for violations.
- Material first-time violations generally receive 7 days' notice and opportunity to cure; severe or repeated violations result in immediate termination.
- We may cooperate with law enforcement and regulators as required by law.

## 7. Reporting Violations

Report suspected violations to `legal@groundworklabs.io`. Include the suspected actor, the nature of the violation, and any supporting evidence.
